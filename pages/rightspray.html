UNDERSTAND THE TREE'S NEEDS

IDENTIFY COMMON DISEASES

Know the pests (e.g., aphids, codling moths) and diseases (e.g., apple scab, fire blight) that commonly affect apple trees in your region.

ASSESS TREE HEALTH

Regularly inspect your trees for signs of infestation or disease. Look for discolored leaves, holes in fruit, and other symptoms.

TYPES OF SPRAYS

INSECTISIDES

Used to control insect pests. Examples include neem oil and pyrethrin.

FUNGICIDES

Used to prevent or control fungal infections. Examples include copper fungicide and sulfur sprays.

HORTICULTURAL MINERAL OILS

Effective against a range of pests by smothering them. Useful for controlling aphids and scale insects.

ORGANIC SPRAYS

Eco-friendly options like insecticidal soaps and natural oils

TIMING GUIDE

DORMANT SEASON SPRAYS

Late Winter: Apply dormant oil sprays to smother overwintering pests.

GROWING SEASON SPRAYS

Pre-Bloom:Apply fungicides to prevent early diseases like apple scab.
Post-Bloom: Use insecticides to control pests like codling moths
Summer Maintenance: Regularly apply sprays to manage ongoing pest and disease pressure.
