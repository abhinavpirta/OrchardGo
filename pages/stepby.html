CHOOSING LOCATION

1.Ensure the site has well-drained soil, full sun (6-8 hours daily), and good air circulation.

2.Avoid frost pockets and low-lying areas prone to cold air.

PREPARE THSE SOIL

1.Test soil pH and amend to maintain a range of 6.0-7.0.
2.Remove weeds and grass in a 3-foot diameter circle around the planting site.

DIG THE HOLE

1.Dig a hole twice the diameter and the same depth as the root ball or bare root.
2.Loosen the soil at the bottom and sides of the hole.

PLANT THE TREE

1.Place the tree in the hole, spreading the roots out evenly.
2.Ensure the graft union (swollen area on the trunk) is 2-3 inches above the soil line.

BACKFILL AND WATER

1.Backfill the hole with native soil, avoiding amendments.
2.Water thoroughly to settle the soil and eliminate air pockets.

MULCH AND PROTECT

1.Apply a 2-3 inch layer of mulch around the tree, keeping it away from the trunk.
2.Install a tree guard to protect the trunk from pests and mechanical damage.

STAKE IF NECESSARY

1.Stake the tree if it's in a windy area or has a weak trunk.
2.Use soft ties and allow some movement to encourage strong root growth.

WATER AND MAINTAIN

1.Water regularly, especially during dry periods, ensuring deep watering.
2.Monitor for pests and diseases, and prune as necessary to promote healthy growth.
