EFFECTIVE PRUNING

Angle Cuts

Make cuts at a 45-degree angle just above a bud facing outward.

Avoid Over Pruning

Regular Pruning

Consistent, annual pruning is better than severe pruning done infrequently.

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

Pruning Too Late

Avoid late-season pruning, which can stimulate growth that won't harden before winter.

Tool Maintenance

Dirty or dull tools can cause ragged cuts and increase disease risk.

Over Pruning

Excessive pruning can weaken the tree and reduce fruit yield.

Remove Dead, Diseased, or Damaged Wood

Identify and cut away any branches that are dead, diseased, or damaged.
Helps prevent the spread of pests and diseases.

Thin Out the Canopy

Remove any crossing or rubbing branches to improve air circulation.
Ensure sunlight can reach all parts of the tree.

Shape the Tree

Cut back branches to maintain a desirable shape and size.
Focus on creating an open, vase-like structure for optimal light penetration.

Remove Suckers and Water Sprouts

Cut off any suckers (shoots from the base) and water sprouts (vertical shoots from branches).
These can divert energy away from fruit production.
