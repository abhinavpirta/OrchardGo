Select Rootstock and Scion

Choose healthy, compatible rootstock and scion wood.
Ensure the scion wood has several buds and is disease-free.

Make Precise Cuts

Use sharp, clean tools to make precise cuts on both the rootstock and scion.
Ensure the cuts match perfectly to allow for a good union.

Join and Secure

Align the cut surfaces of the rootstock and scion.
Secure them tightly using grafting tape or rubber bands to hold them in place.

Seal and Protect:

Apply grafting wax or a sealant to protect the graft from moisture loss and infection.
Cover the graft with a protective bag or wrap if necessary.

Monitor and Maintain

Regularly check the graft for signs of success or failure.
Ensure the area remains clean and free from pests or diseases.
